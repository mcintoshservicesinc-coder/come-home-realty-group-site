Why Home Watch matters

When a property sits empty, small issues can turn into expensive repairs. Regular in-person checks help catch problems early and give owners peace of mind while away.

Common problems when homes sit empty

Many homeowners are surprised how quickly small issues can develop when a property is left unattended.

Plumbing

Dry plumbing traps

Toilets and sinks that sit unused can dry out, allowing sewer gases and odors into the home.

AC

HVAC problems

Air conditioning issues can go unnoticed for weeks and create humidity or mold concerns.

Storms

Storm damage

After strong storms, small roof or exterior problems can become expensive repairs if unnoticed.

Pests

Pest activity

Empty homes are more likely to attract insects or rodents without regular monitoring.

Water

Small leaks

Minor plumbing leaks can cause significant damage when left undetected for long periods.

Security

Visible vacancy

Packages, flyers, or overgrown lawns can signal that a property is unoccupied.

Home Watch inspection checklist

This makes the service look professional to clients and helps keep every visit consistent.

Exterior

  • Front door secure
  • Rear and side doors secure
  • Windows appear secure
  • No visible storm damage
  • No concerning pest activity
  • No unexpected flyers or buildup
  • No standing water observed
  • No obvious exterior damage noted

Interior

  • Flush all toilets
  • Run sinks and showers briefly
  • No visible leaks under sinks
  • AC operating normally
  • Thermostat set properly
  • No unusual odors
  • No visible mold or moisture concerns
  • Appliances and breakers appear normal
